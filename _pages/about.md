Hello! I'm a Research Data Scientist at [RTI International](rti.org)'s [Center for Data Science](https://www.rti.org/centers/rti-center-data-science). I'm also a PhD Candidate in Informatics at Indiana University's [Center for Complex Networks and Systems (CNetS)](https://cnets.indiana.edu/phd/) working primarily with the [Observatory on Social Media (OSoMe)](https://osome.iuni.iu.edu/). 

My PhD work is focused on measuring memetic and behavioral effects of interacting with social media content. One project focuses on assessing the impact of antivaccine tweets on vaccine uptake during the 2021 COVID vaccine rollout, using the [CoVaxxy](https://osome.iu.edu/tools/covaxxy) dataset I previously contributed to building. [Another project](https://epjdatascience.springeropen.com/articles/10.1140/epjds/s13688-022-00364-4) looks at how legislative discourse in the UK Parliament follows the topics discussed on social media. The last project measures the impact of interacting with social media posts on individual's attention to topics and stated sentiments toward named entities.  Overall, I find that social media does have impacts on political discourse, public health outcomes, and individual attention and sentiments. 

At RTI, I aim to work primarily in the domains of public health and climate change.  My first project with RTI is examining text data from electronic health records (EHR) to characterize long-COVID and its impacts and prevelence.
